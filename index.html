The Belief

Perfection is a myth.
Excellence is a choice.

Most self-help is secretly about perfection. It sets a standard you can never reach, then makes you feel like a failure for not reaching it. That's not improvement — that's a trap.

HelpingMe99 is built on a different idea: 99% is the highest standard any of us can actually live. Showing up on the tired Thursday. The honest conversation you have anyway. The budget you stick to, mostly. That's not settling — that's how real change happens.

Every book in this collection is built on behavioral science and honest tools — no faith required, no willpower required. Just systems that work with your brain.

What we stand for

Three things every book is built on

🔬

Science, not faith

Every recommendation traces back to real research — behavioral psychology, neuroscience, habit science. No pseudoscience, no guesswork.

⚙️

Systems over willpower

Willpower is finite. It runs out when you're tired, stressed, or triggered — exactly when you need it most. Good systems don't run out.

✦

99% is enough

Perfectionism is the reason most people quit. Consistent, imperfect progress — 99% — always beats a perfect plan that never starts.

How HelpingMe99 is different

I

Written for reality, not ideal conditions

Every guide includes what to do when you slip — because most people do, and what you do in the next ten minutes determines everything. No book here assumes you'll execute it perfectly.

II

No moralising, no shame

You're not weak. You're not broken. You've just been given the wrong tools. These books don't lecture — they equip.

III

Short enough to actually finish

No padding. No filler. Each book says what it needs to say and stops. You'll read them, not display them on a shelf.

The Author

99 HelpingMe99

Paul Arbour

I write practical guides because I couldn't find the ones I needed. The quit-smoking books were built on willpower that runs out. The recovery resources assumed a 12-step model that doesn't work for everyone. The self-improvement books were secretly about perfection.

So I went back to the science — behavioral psychology, neuroscience, habit research — and wrote the books I wished had existed. Everything lives under HelpingMe99, because 99% is the highest standard any of us can actually live. And it's enough.
